Lebanon

Lebanon is in the middle east on the eastern side of the mediterranean sea. It borders Syria to the north and east and Israel to the south. Although it is in the middle east, Lebanon is not a country full of hot sandy deserts, but mountains cover the country. The three largest cities in Lebanon are Tripoli, Beirut, and Sidon, with its capital being Beirut. Lebanon is a cultural melting pot, filled with people from different religions and cultures. Arabic is the most popular language, and Islam is the most common religion.

People and Society

Languages

The official language is Arabic, but French, English, and Armenian are popular as well.

Population

As of 2022 the population is 5,296,814.

Ethnic Groups

The majority of Lebanon is Arab with 95%, and Armenian at 4%, while other ethnic groups making up the 1%.

Geography

Location

Lebanon is in the Middle East. It borders Syria to the north and east and Israel to the south.

Climate

Lebanon has a Mediterranean climate, which includes hot and dry summers and cold and rainy winters. In the Spring time, it is possible to swim along the coast and go skiing in the mountains.

Elevation

Lebanon's highest peak elevation is Qurnat al-Sawdāʾ. It is in northern Lebanon and is 3,088 meters. Therefore Lebanon is one of the highest countries in the world. Lebanon's lowest elevation level is the Mediterranean sea at 0 meters.

Government

Government Type

Lebanon has a parliamentary republic type of Government.

Capital

Beirut is the capital of Lebanon and the largest city. Beirut has a population of 2.4 million, therefore it is the third-largest city in the Levant region.

Population distribution

In Lebanon, most people live near the Mediterranean coast. Most specifically, Beirut, which is the capital.
